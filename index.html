MIGIS: Meaningful Intelligence in GIS

About Me

Lingbo Liu is a research associate at the Center for Geographic Analysis (CGA), Harvard University. He was a CGA postdoctoral research fellow, and previously a tenured lecturer in the Department of Urban Planning, School of Urban Design, Wuhan University, and a visiting scholar at Harvard CGA. He earned his doctoral degree in Urban and Rural Planning with a focus on digital urban administration and planning from Wuhan University in 2018. At CGA, Lingbo leads both the Spatial Data Lab and the Geospatial Analytics Visual Programming initiative, focusing on the development of advanced geospatial models, the integration of modular spatial modeling capabilities into visual analytics platforms, and open-source tools and training programs to democratize geospatial AI (GeoAI) research. His research focuses on the study of healthy cities, utilizing advanced GeoAI models to capture the spatiotemporal features of urban systems. He aims to parse the coupling mechanisms of the space-human system to provide decision support for public policy to make cities healthier.
